What is Microblading?

This technique is a process that may be used to improve or create definition of the brow. Microblading can be used to cover gaps of lost hair, to extend the eyebrows, or may be used as a full reconstruction if the brows have little to no hair. Each microblading stroke is applied individually, allowing me to control the shape, color and density of the completed eyebrows.

What is a Microblade?

Microblading is done with a tool called a microblade… it is a manual pen shaped tool that has a collection of tiny needles arranged in a specific way to help the artist create each, individual hair stroke.

Will it hurt?

Everyones pain tolerance is different, so it is hard to say if this procedure will hurt you. Before the microblading starts, every client is numbed with an agent for 20-30 minutes prior to starting. I would say 90% of clients feel little to no pain throughout the microblading session. Once the first pass (placing the outline of strokes into the skin) is complete, another numbing agent is placed onto the skin to make sure you are completely comfortable. For those that have felt a little discomfort have described the pain similar to a cat scratch, or scratching a sun burn.

Must Read!

  • Microblading is a semi-permanent procedure. Although most Microblading applications are successfull, there is NO GUARANTEE that a specific client will have depth of color retention or retention at all. Some clients may not benefit from this procedure at all.

  • Microblading is the process of inserting pigment into the basal layer of the epidermis. It is a form of tattooing, although semi-permanent. Generally results are excellent, but do vary depending on your skin texture/oiliness/scars etc. Scars may not retain ink well or possibly at all. This is a semi-permanent procedure and perfect results are not a realistic expectation.

  • Initially the color will be very vibrant and strokes will be precise and sharp. 3-7 days after microblading the color will fade 40-50%. The pigmentation will soften and look more natural.

  • Uneven pigmentation can result from poor healing, infection, excessive bleeding during blading or just my skin typing. Everyones healing process is different. Sometimes how you care for your new brows affect the end result. There are cases where poor results are a direct result of skin typing even with excellent aftercare.

  • Scabbing, although undesirable, may happen. A touch up session will be needed to correct my brows if any of these factors were present during my blading session.


Questions or Appointments

What can I expect for my first visit?

The first thing you would do Is fill out a short 2 page medical history form, as well as signing off on an informational sheet that will tell you all the risks that can occur from microblading. After that we would talk shape size and color for your brows. I will answer any and all questions you have, and look at any reference pictures you have for your new brows.

Next is the pre-draw where I do some measurements for symmetry and make sure everything is to your liking then we blade you! Once you love your outline I will numb the area with topical for about 20-30 mins. After the skin is broken (after the first pass/strokes) I use another numbing agent, then on to the microblading!

The entire session with drawing and numbing and talking is about 2-2.5 hours. I will have you check your brows before you leave and make any changes you request, if necessary. An ointment will be placed on your brows and I will go over aftercare instructions before you leave.

How many appointments do I need and how long does it last?

Two sessions are needed to complete microblading. Strokes can fade up to 50% the first session, therefore a second session is important. The second session is scheduled 4-6 weeks out once the brows are completely healed. This is a semi permanent tattoo, so it can last between 12-18 months (depending on how well you take care of them). I recommend a touch up at least once a year to keep the color crisp, and the shape clean and defined.

Do I need to do anything different before my appointment?

Prior to your appointment there are some things you need to avoid. Alcohol causes excessive bleeding which pushes pigment out of the skin which will affect the retention of ink once the brows heal. I also suggest avoiding caffeine and blood thinners for the same reasons listed above! If botox, Retin-A, Glycolic acid or fillers of any kind are done prior to your appointment, please let me know so we can schedule accordingly.
